MIDI Exercise : don't forget to team up !




As explained before in the video, in this exercise you will need to put the midi notes to the correct height. Decide which one of you will be the "listener" (i.e. the person that can hear the results) while the other one will be the "maestro" (i.e. the person in charge of moving the midi notes around and receiving indications from the listener). Don't forget to plug an headset to your computer (if you don't have any, please ask the supervisor(s) for one) as only the listener can hear sounds from the computer. The listener should not have any visual access to the computer screen, while the maestro should not be able to hear what the listener hears.

Once you have settled who does what, and read this small paragraph, you can start the exercise. On this mp3 web player below can be heard what should be the expected result. The listener can ask to listen to this extract as many times as he wants. Clicking on the link below the mp3 player will lead you to a website where a sequence of MIDI notes are displayed. The maestro can alter the height of the notes simply by dragging the boxes on the height he wants. On the left upper corner can be found a small play button which will play the sequence to the listener. Again, the listener can ask as many times as he wants to listen to what the maestro has been doing on the website. The listener should give constructive feedback and indicatiosn to the maestro in order to reconstruct the original tune. The listener should neither tell what is the song he is hearing (if he recognizes it) neither sing it to the maestro. Good luck, and have fun !
